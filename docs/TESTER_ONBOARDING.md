# Tester Onboarding Guide
*AlvaradoTech-MCP | You bring the idea. The stack executes it.*

---

## What You're Testing

You've been invited to test a self-hosted AI execution platform.

Here's what that means in plain terms: you send a message to a Telegram bot. The bot plans the task, asks for your approval, executes it on real hardware, and reports back. No cloud. No subscription. Runs on hardware sitting in a home office in the Phoenix metro, AZ.

Your job: bring an idea. A real one. Something you actually want built or automated. We'll use this platform to develop and execute it.

---

## What You Need

- A smartphone or computer with Telegram installed
- That's it.

---

## Step 1 — Get Added

Contact Rob Alvarado and tell him you're ready. He'll:

1. Add your Telegram user ID to the platform
2. Send you the bot link
3. Confirm you're paired and active

You don't need to install anything on your own machine. The entire stack runs on Rob's hardware.

---

## Step 2 — The First Conversation

Your first conversation with MCP is intentional. Before it helps you with anything, it will ask you a series of questions.

This is not a form. It is a conversation.

MCP wants to understand who you are, how you think, and what you are actually trying to solve. Not the surface version — the real one. It will push gently if your answers are thin. That is on purpose.

The quality of every future response depends on this first conversation. The more honest you are, the sharper MCP gets. Output relies on input.

Expect 5-10 minutes. It only happens once.

If you get no response within 60 seconds of your first message — tell Rob. Something's down.

---

## Step 3 — Bring Your Idea

This is the important part.

Don't test with toy examples. Bring a real idea:

- A script you've always wanted automated
- A file organization task
- A report you want generated on a schedule
- A monitoring task for your own systems
- A workflow you do manually that you want the agent to handle

The more real it is, the more useful the test.

---

## How the Execution Loop Works

1. **You describe the task** in plain language via Telegram
2. **MCP builds a plan** — breaks it into steps, lists what it will do
3. **MCP asks for your approval** before doing anything
4. **You say YES** (or NO, or modify the plan)
5. **MCP executes** — runs the commands on the hardware
6. **MCP reports back** — tells you what happened, confirms completion

You are always in control. Nothing runs without your approval.

---

## What MCP Can Do

- Create, read, edit, and organize files
- Run shell commands and scripts
- Search the web
- Report system status
- Execute multi-step workflows
- Maintain memory of your sessions

---

## What to Report Back

After each test session, tell Rob:

1. What you asked for
2. Whether it worked
3. What was confusing or broken
4. What you wish it could do that it couldn't

Your feedback directly shapes the next build iteration.

---

## If Something Breaks

Tell Rob via Telegram or directly. Include:
- What you sent the bot
- What it responded (or didn't)
- Approximate time

The platform has a watchdog — Rob gets alerted if machines go down. But specific task failures need your report.

---

## Ground Rules

- This is a test platform. Don't send tasks that involve your personal credentials, private data, or anything you wouldn't want written to a file on someone else's hardware.
- The agent has access to the hardware it runs on. It does not have access to your devices.
- Approval is required before execution. Read the plan before you say yes.

---

## The Bigger Picture

This platform is designed to be cloned. The goal is a replicable base that anyone can set up on their own hardware — no cloud, no ongoing cost, full control.

Your test helps prove that model. If it works for you with your real ideas, it works for anyone.
